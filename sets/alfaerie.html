Alfaerie Variant Chess Graphics

The following is my own attempt to come up with some graphics for chess variant (or fairy chess) pieces. The desirability of such designs are almost always a matter of personal preference, so I'm not trying to imply that any of the pre-existing graphics are lacking. I really like the Chess Alpha font (designed by Eric Bentzen), so naturally I wanted a set of graphics that would match up well with that font.

The graphics shown below constitute a set of chess variant pieces which I have somewhat pretentiously named the Alfaerie collection. I've made an effort to be keep the pieces simple and have them match up well with the existing Alpha Chess font. The "Animal" pieces are all profiles facing left, with only the head and neck shown. Due to my limited artistic abilities, they do not look as nice as the Knight, but I've tried to come close.

The intent here is not to assign graphics to certain pieces. For example, the piece that looks like a Unicorn could be used as a Bishop+Nightrider, or as some other piece such as a 3D unicorn piece. Or the piece that's a graphical combination of Nightrider and Bishop could be used as a Bishop+Nightrider. The name or graphic used for a piece in this set does not necessarily correlate to a particular piece or movement capability.

This collection is admitedly geared toward those comfortable with western style chess pieces. I will be attempting to add more pieces as time permits.

You may download and use the Alfaerie graphics if you wish. The .zip file contains bitmaps for using the pieces with Zillions of Games, and also GIF files with transparent backgrounds. It also includes various Zillions of Games rules files and some web pages documenting these games. Please remember to check the 'use folder names' checkbox when you extract the files, and extract them to your zillions folder (if you have one). download alfaerie.zip

Notes

  • The standard chess piece graphics were derived directly from Eric Bentzen's Chess Alpha font.
  • The Lion graphic is based on John William Brown's Murray Lion graphic used in Centennial Chess.
  • The Cannon and Vao graphics utilized Fergus Duniho's idea of horizontal and diagonal oriented cannon figures. The idea for using the Rook and Bishop as the basis for these shapes is my own.
  • The Gold and Silver general figures utilize Fergus Duniho's idea of using alchemical symbols for Gold and Silver.
